Welcome

I am an atmospheric scientist studying how human activities shape air quality and climate, and how communities can respond to these changes. My research combines fine-scale sensor observations, numerical modeling, and statistical analysis to improve our understanding of urban greenhouse gas and air pollutant emissions.

Currently, I am developing calibration methods for dense sensor networks and using enhancement ratios (ΔNOx/ΔCO₂, ΔCO/ΔCO₂, ΔCH₄/ΔCO₂) to infer sector-specific emission factors in multiple U.S. cities.

Looking ahead, I am interested in applying advanced AI/ML approaches to quantify Scope 1 emissions, with potential extensions to Scope 2 and Scope 3 categories. I am also keen to investigate biogenic CO₂ uptake by urban vegetation to better understand the role of cities in the carbon cycle.
